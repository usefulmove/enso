# STORY-004: LinkedIn — Featured Post

## Goal
Write and publish a LinkedIn featured post that introduces "the emacs of agent orchestrators"
framing and the ensoOS vision. Startup founder voice. This becomes the canonical public
statement of what we're building.

## Acceptance Criteria
- [x] Post draft written in startup founder voice
- [x] Key phrases included naturally:
  - "the emacs of agent orchestrators"
  - "participates in the construction of its own operational context"
  - "software building software"
  - "ensoOS"
- [x] Post drives engagement and signals what you're building to the right audience
- [x] Draft reviewed and approved before publishing
- [ ] Post published and pinned as featured on LinkedIn profile (user executing)
- [ ] Linked from STORY-002 (headline + about) for narrative consistency

## Approach & Verification Plan

### Steps
1. **Read STORY-002** — Ensure the post extends and is consistent with headline/about narrative
2. **Draft post structure:**
   - **Hook** (1-2 lines) — grab attention immediately. Bold claim or provocative question.
   - **The insight** — what you discovered / what you're building
   - **The metaphor** — "the emacs of agent orchestrators" with brief context
   - **The key phrase** — "agents that participate in the construction of their own operational context"
   - **The vision** — ensoOS, the agent operating system, where this is going
   - **The invitation** — who should follow along / reach out / collaborate
3. **Tone calibration** — Founder voice: confident, a little audacious, genuinely excited, not hype
4. **Length** — LinkedIn posts perform best at 150-300 words for thought leadership
5. **Formatting** — Short paragraphs, white space, no bullet walls
6. **Review** — Present draft for approval
7. **Execute** — Deliver final copy-paste ready post
8. **Pin** — Instructions for pinning as featured post on profile
9. **Verify** — Confirm post published, pinned, and rendering correctly

### Risks & Unknowns
- "Emacs" reference may need brief contextual bridge for non-technical audience
- LinkedIn algorithm favors native content — no external links in body (add in comments)
- Post must feel authentic, not like a press release
- Timing matters — post when your audience is active

### Verification
- Post reads as authentic founder voice, not AI-generated
- Key phrases land naturally, not forced
- Narrative arc is clear: insight → metaphor → vision → invitation
- Pinned as featured post on LinkedIn profile
- Engagement (comments, shares) validates the message landed
