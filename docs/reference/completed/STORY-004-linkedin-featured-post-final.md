Emacs isn't just an editor. It's a Lisp machine that became whatever its users needed it to be.

That's what I'm building for AI agents.

**enso** is the emacs of agent orchestrators. An agent harness where software builds software.

Most agents today are black boxes: you prompt, they respond, end of story. But the agents that will matter are the ones that **participate in the construction of their own operational context** — discovering capabilities, composing new behaviors, extending themselves.

This is **ensoOS**: not a tool, but a living system. Agents that don't just execute tasks but understand the task space. Agents that compound their own abilities over time.

The emacs parallel runs deep. Both are:
- Self-extending by design
- Built around a simple, powerful abstraction (Lisp / agent orchestration)
- Used by the people who build the future

I'm sharing the work openly. enso is on GitHub. The protocol is documented. The vision is **software building software**.

If you're thinking about self-improving systems, agent architectures, or how AI actually gets integrated into complex workflows — let's talk. The infrastructure layer is being built now, and the patterns aren't obvious yet.
